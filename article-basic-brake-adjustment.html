Basic Brake Adjustment

Keep your brakes responsive and safe

Why This Matters on E-Bikes

E-bikes are heavier and faster than regular bikes. Your brakes work harder and wear faster. Properly adjusted brakes aren't optional — they're critical safety equipment.

Know Your Brake Type

Rim Brakes (V-brakes, Caliper)

  • Brake pads squeeze the wheel rim
  • Common on older/budget e-bikes
  • Easier to adjust and maintain
  • Less effective in wet conditions

Mechanical Disc Brakes

  • Cable-actuated, pads squeeze a rotor
  • Common on mid-range e-bikes
  • Better wet weather performance
  • Requires periodic adjustment as pads wear

Hydraulic Disc Brakes

  • Fluid-actuated, self-adjusting
  • Standard on quality e-bikes
  • Minimal adjustment needed
  • Require bleeding (fluid replacement) occasionally

Signs Your Brakes Need Attention

  • Lever pulls too far before braking starts
  • Squealing or grinding sounds
  • Reduced stopping power compared to when new
  • Pulsing or vibration when braking
  • Lever feels spongy (hydraulic brakes)

Rim Brake Adjustment

Adjusting Pad Position

  1. Loosen the pad mounting bolt (don't remove it)
  2. Squeeze the brake lever to center the pad on the rim
  3. Pad should hit flat on the rim, not the tire
  4. Tighten the bolt while holding position
  5. Release lever, check alignment

Tightening the Cable

If the lever pulls too far before engaging:

  1. Find the barrel adjuster (where cable meets lever)
  2. Turn it counterclockwise to take up slack
  3. Test lever feel
  4. If you run out of adjustment, you may need to re-clamp the cable

When to Replace Pads

Look for wear indicator grooves. If grooves are gone, replace pads.

Mechanical Disc Brake Adjustment

Centering the Caliper

If you hear rubbing:

  1. Loosen the two caliper mounting bolts (don't remove)
  2. Squeeze the brake lever firmly, hold it
  3. While holding lever, tighten the bolts
  4. Release lever, spin wheel — should be silent

Pad Adjustment

Most mechanical disc brakes have adjustment for each pad:

  1. Inner pad: Adjusted via lever cable tension
  2. Outer pad: Turn the adjustment knob on the caliper
  3. Adjust outer pad close to rotor without rubbing
  4. Tighten cable so lever engages at desired point

Hydraulic Disc Brake Adjustment

Good news: these self-adjust as pads wear. Bad news: when something's wrong, it's more complex.

What You Can Do

  • Lever reach adjustment: Most have a dial or screw at the lever to adjust how far the lever sits from the bar
  • Centering the caliper: Same process as mechanical — loosen bolts, squeeze lever, tighten bolts

What Needs a Shop

  • Spongy lever feel → needs bleeding (air in the lines)
  • Leaking fluid → seal replacement
  • Contaminated pads → pad replacement (don't touch brake surfaces with bare hands)

Rotor Care

Keep Them Clean

  • Don't touch rotors with bare hands (oils reduce braking)
  • Clean with isopropyl alcohol and clean rag
  • Never use WD-40 or lubricants near brakes
Important: Minimum rotor thickness is stamped on the rotor (usually 1.5mm). If worn below this, replace immediately.

Bedding In New Pads

New brake pads need to be "bedded in" for full performance:

  1. Find a safe area with room to brake
  2. Accelerate to moderate speed (15 mph)
  3. Brake firmly but not locked until nearly stopped
  4. Repeat 10-20 times
  5. Avoid dragging brakes during this process

This transfers pad material evenly to the rotor surface.

When to Go to a Shop

  • Hydraulic brake bleeding
  • Persistent squealing after cleaning/adjustment
  • Brake lever goes to bar even after adjustment
  • Any crash damage to brake components
  • You're not confident in your work
Safety first: E-bike brakes are safety-critical. There's no shame in getting professional help.
