Group Ride Etiquette

How to ride safely and courteously in a pack

Why This Matters

Group riding has different dynamics than solo riding. A mistake doesn't just affect you — it can take down other riders. Good etiquette keeps everyone safe and makes rides enjoyable.

Before the Ride

Know the Ride Style

  • No-drop: Group waits for everyone, no one left behind
  • Regroup: Faster riders wait at intersections
  • Race pace: Keep up or get dropped

Ask beforehand so you know what to expect.

Check Your Bike

A mechanical on a group ride affects everyone. Before you roll:

  • Brakes working properly
  • Tires inflated
  • Chain lubed
  • Battery charged (e-bike!)

Be On Time

Late arrivals disrupt the group. Arrive 10-15 minutes early to prep.

Riding Position

Hold Your Line

  • Ride in a straight, predictable line
  • Don't swerve around small debris
  • No sudden movements
  • The riders behind you are trusting your line

Maintain Spacing

  • Following distance: Half to full wheel length
  • Too close = risk of overlap and crash
  • Too far = gaps that stretch the group
Wheel Overlap Warning: Never overlap your front wheel with the rear wheel in front of you. If they swerve, your wheels touch — you go down, not them. Stay directly behind or fully alongside.

Side by Side

  • Two abreast maximum on roads
  • Single file when cars need to pass
  • Move right when you hear "car back"

Communication

Verbal Calls

Standard calls everyone should know:

Call Meaning
"Car back!"Vehicle approaching from behind
"Car up!"Vehicle approaching from front
"On your left!"Passing on the left
"Slowing!"About to brake
"Stopping!"Coming to a stop
"Hole!" / "Gravel!"Hazard in the road
"Clear!" / "Not clear!"Intersection status
"Single up!"Get in single file
"Rolling!"Starting to move
Pass calls back. If you hear "car back," relay it forward so the whole group knows.

Hand Signals

  • Slowing: Hand down, palm facing back
  • Stopping: Fist behind back
  • Hazard left/right: Point down at the hazard
  • Turn: Point in direction of turn
  • Single file: One finger raised behind back

Point at hazards early enough for following riders to react.

E-Bike Specific Etiquette

Power Moderation

  • Don't surge ahead on uphills — stay with the group
  • Match the pace, not your motor's capability
  • Use lower assist levels to match non-e-bikes if needed

Be Aware of Your Weight

E-bikes are heavier. In a crash:

  • More momentum to control
  • Harder to stop quickly
  • More impact if you hit something

Give yourself extra following distance.

Speed Matching

If riding with non-electric bikes:

  • Your assist makes uphills easy; others are working hard
  • Don't sprint off and wait — it disrupts group flow
  • Use your motor to maintain steady pace, not to show off

Common Mistakes

Braking in the Pack

  • Avoid sudden braking — riders behind can't react fast enough
  • Use verbal warning ("Slowing!")
  • Soft pedaling slows you without drama

Half-Wheeling

  • Riding just ahead of the person beside you
  • Forces them to speed up to stay alongside
  • Match their pace, not slightly ahead

Attacking (Surging Ahead)

  • Unless it's that kind of ride, don't surge off the front
  • It breaks up the group and exhausts riders who chase

Headphones

  • Don't wear them, or keep one ear free
  • You need to hear calls and cars

If You Crash or Get Dropped

Crash

  • Call out so others stop
  • Move yourself and bike off the road if safe
  • Group should stop — no one left behind (in no-drop rides)

Mechanical

  • Call out and pull over safely
  • In regroup rides, others will catch up
  • Carry basic tools so you're not stranded

Can't Keep Up

  • Let the group know ("go ahead, I'll catch up at the next stop")
  • No shame — different fitness levels are normal
  • Regroup rides account for this

Starting a Group Ride

If you're organizing:

  • Clearly state ride type (no-drop, pace, distance)
  • Set expectations for e-bikes if relevant
  • Designate a sweep rider (stays at back, no one behind them)
  • Brief new riders on hand signals and calls
